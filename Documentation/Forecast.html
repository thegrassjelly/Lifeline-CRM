Regression Modeling


The Regression Modeling tool seen in the Forecast submodule lets administrators use previous data used in the systems database to create regression analysis of the data selected.


A link to a full transcript of documentation to how to create this linear regression model can be seen in this link:


http://www.telerik.com/blogs/how-to-plot-a-simple-linear-regression-in-telerik-asp.net-web-form-chart


In a brief summary of the transcript, basically the model is referred to the approach in statistics related to modeling the relationship between a dependent variable/variables and an independent variable. Regression lines are also known as trend lines, and to which there are different types such as linear, logarithmic, exponential and so on. The main trend lines used in the page is the linear.


Going back to the Regression modeling tool, to use it the administrator picks a period with ranges from 10, 20 and 30. This numbers are representations of dates on the dataset, which the user can pick either # of Renewals over a period and Payment amounts.


The span gets the span of the most recent records depending on the chosen selection. A span of 7 gets the recent 7 days dataset, 30 days gets the recent

30 day dataset on the time of using the tool, and so on.


After modeling the trend line, administrators or management then can pick the appropriate regression type closest to recreate the most accurate forecast with the formula formulated in the right.
